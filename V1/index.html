Corticosteroid Ladder

Glucocorticoid potency and equivalent dosing

Steroid Relative Potency Equivalent Dose Duration Clinical Uses
Betamethasone 25-30x 0.75 mg 36-54h Fetal lung maturation, severe inflammation
Dexamethasone 25-30x 0.75 mg 36-54h Cerebral oedema, croup, severe asthma, COVID-19
Methylprednisolone 5x 4 mg 12-36h Acute asthma, COPD, spinal cord injury
Prednisolone 4x 5 mg 12-36h Asthma, COPD, polymyalgia rheumatica, IBD
Hydrocortisone 1x (baseline) 20 mg 8-12h Addison's, adrenal crisis, septic shock
Cortisone 0.8x 25 mg 8-12h Rarely used (requires hepatic conversion)

High Potency (Long-acting)

Dexamethasone, Betamethasone

  • Minimal mineralocorticoid activity
  • Suitable for cerebral oedema
  • Used in fetal lung maturation
  • Severe inflammatory conditions

Intermediate Potency

Prednisolone, Methylprednisolone

  • Most common for systemic use
  • Acute asthma exacerbations
  • COPD exacerbations
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • IBD flares

Low Potency (Short-acting)

Hydrocortisone

  • Physiological replacement therapy
  • Addison's disease
  • Adrenal crisis
  • Has mineralocorticoid activity

Clinical Pearls

  • Conversion: Prednisolone 40mg Hydrocortisone 100mg Dexamethasone 6mg
  • Tapering: Required if >3 weeks use, >40mg/day prednisolone, or evening doses
  • Side effects: Hyperglycaemia, immunosuppression, osteoporosis, adrenal suppression
  • Morning dosing: Mimics physiological cortisol rhythm (except dexamethasone)
  • PPI protection: Consider if high-dose, elderly, or GI risk factors

WHO Analgesic Ladder

Stepwise approach to pain management

Step 3

Severe Pain (7-10/10)

Strong Opioids Non-opioid Adjuvant

Morphine 5-10mg PO/2.5-5mg SC/IV 4-hourly (immediate release) Gold standard, predictable
Oxycodone 5-10mg PO 4-6 hourly 1.5x potency of morphine
Fentanyl 12-25mcg/hr patch (change 72-hourly) 100x morphine potency, for stable pain
Buprenorphine 5-20mcg/hr patch (change weekly) Partial agonist, ceiling effect
Step 2

Moderate Pain (4-6/10)

Weak Opioids Non-opioid Adjuvant

Codeine 30-60mg PO 4-6 hourly (max 240mg/day) Prodrug, 10% morphine potency
Tramadol 50-100mg PO/IV 6-hourly (max 400mg/day) Mixed mechanism, lower dependence risk
Dihydrocodeine 30mg PO 4-6 hourly Similar to codeine
Step 1

Mild Pain (1-3/10)

Non-opioid Adjuvant

Paracetamol 1g PO/IV 6-hourly (max 4g/day) Safe, minimal side effects
Ibuprofen 400mg PO 8-hourly (max 2.4g/day) Anti-inflammatory, GI risk
Naproxen 250-500mg PO 12-hourly Longer-acting NSAID
Diclofenac 50mg PO 8-hourly Potent NSAID, CV risk

Adjuvant Analgesics (All Steps)

Neuropathic Pain
  • Amitriptyline: 10-75mg nocte
  • Gabapentin: 300mg-1.2g TDS
  • Pregabalin: 75-300mg BD
  • Duloxetine: 60mg OD
Bone Pain
  • Bisphosphonates (e.g., zoledronic acid)
  • Radiotherapy for metastases
  • NSAIDs (if safe)
Muscle Spasm
  • Baclofen: 5-20mg TDS
  • Diazepam: 2-5mg BD-TDS
Inflammatory Pain
  • Corticosteroids (e.g., dexamethasone 4-8mg OD)

Opioid Conversion Guide

Medication Route Oral Morphine Equivalent
Morphine Oral 1 (baseline)
Morphine SC/IV 2-3x oral dose
Oxycodone Oral 1.5-2x
Codeine Oral 10
Tramadol Oral 10
Fentanyl patch Transdermal 12mcg/hr 30mg oral morphine/day
Buprenorphine patch Transdermal 5mcg/hr 12mg oral morphine/day

Clinical Pearls

  • Breakthrough pain: 1/6th of total daily opioid dose for rescue
  • Starting strong opioids: Morphine 5mg PO 4-hourly or 2.5mg SC 4-hourly
  • Titration: Increase by 30-50% if inadequate pain control
  • Constipation: ALL patients on opioids need laxatives (senna + docusate)
  • Nausea: Common in first week, prescribe antiemetic prophylactically
  • Renal impairment: Avoid morphine, codeine (toxic metabolites); use oxycodone, fentanyl, buprenorphine
  • Hepatic impairment: Reduce doses, avoid codeine
  • By the mouth: Oral route preferred when possible
  • By the clock: Regular dosing, not PRN alone
  • Multimodal: Combine paracetamol + NSAID + opioid for additive effect
